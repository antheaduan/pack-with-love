Who We Are

An independent, youth-led service club where creativity, teamwork, and compassion become real care for children and communities.

Our story

Founded by a sixth grader with a big heart

Pack with Love Youth Service Club was founded by Joanna Zhang as a sixth-grade student, with a simple desire: to help children feel loved, seen, and remembered.

Designed primarily for elementary and middle school students, the club gives young people a meaningful place to create, serve, learn, and grow as caring leaders. As it grows, high school students may join as members, mentors, and project leaders under adult-advisor guidance.

We partner — project by project — with churches, schools, nonprofits, and mission programs, while remaining fully independent and youth-led.

🎯

Our Mission

To help young people use creativity, teamwork, and care to create practical and meaningful projects for children — in partnership with trusted local communities.

✨

A Double Purpose

To care for children and communities, and to help young people grow through meaningful service. We don't measure success only by how many items we give.

Our foundation

Rooted in Christian love, compassion, and service

We believe every child is created with God-given dignity and deserves to be seen, valued, encouraged, and cared for. We seek to reflect the love of Jesus through joyful creation, thoughtful service, and practical care — always with humility, kindness, and respect.

We serve children and families of every background with respect. No child or family is ever required to attend an activity, share personal information, or take part in a follow-up program in order to receive a backpack or care item.

What we promise

The promises we keep for every project

  • Listen First

    We learn about the children and the setting before deciding what to create.

  • Create with Care

    We choose designs and items that are safe, useful, joyful, and culturally respectful.

  • Keep Children at the Center

    Children are people to love, never a photo opportunity.

  • Support Trusted Partners

    Our work strengthens the relationships local partners already have with families.

  • Grow Young Leaders

    Students carry real, age-appropriate responsibility; adults hold the guardrails.

  • Learn Before We Grow

    We reflect and improve before taking on bigger quantities or new partners.
